Introduction

To start of with, tennis is a sport that is played with a racquet, rubber balls covered in felt. It can be played against a single opponent or in a 2v2 fashion, called doubles, by men, women or mixed doubles. There is also wheelchair tennis, which allows players who are movement impaired to be able to play the sport. The way you score points in tennis is by scoring a winner (the opponet is unable to touch to get their racquet on the ball), or through the opponent's error, forced or unforced - it is considered an error, when a player plays the ball outside of the court limitations (called an "out"), or when a player plays the ball into the net that divides the court (considered a "fault").

Tennis is a very movement heavy sport, where your legs and body are always in constant movement. Because you need to have fast reaction times, due to the constant change of direction that the ball takes, tennis players cannot afford to stay still. Due to this, the main muscle groups a person uses while playing tennis, are the core and the legs. Tennis can also place a lot of strain on your arm muscles, due to the amount of spin and power that the modern game requires.


Forehand

The forehand is most players' strongest weapon. Most players' forehands are hit with one hand. For a right-handed player, the forehand is a stroke that begins on the right side of the body, continues across the body as contact is made with the ball, and ends on the left side of the body, in an upwards motion. Due to advance in racquet technology, today, forehands are hit with an incredible amount of spin. This allows players to hit the ball quite hard whie still falling inside the court.

Some of the greatest forehands are known for their incredible placement, power and spin. For example, the forehands of:

  • Roger Federer
  • Rafael Nadal

Backhand

A tennis player's backhand can be one-handed or two-handed. Your stance is very important when hitting a backhand, this is especially true for the one-handed backhand.

It is easier to generate pace and spin with a one-handed backhand. However, two handed backhands tend to be more consistent.

Not a lot of players can boast about their backhand, however some of the great backhands of our time include:

  • Ivan Lendl
  • Andre Agassi
  • Novak Djokovic

Serve

A tennis point always starts with a serve, and a good serve can give you a lot of free points. In tennis a server gets two tries to put the ball inside the service box, diagonally - a first and second serve.

For this reason, a players first serve is usually very powerful, always trying to go for an ace or for an easy follow-up shot. The modern-second serve is usually a kick serve, infused with as much spin as possible, so it's easy to go over the net while making it hard for the receiver to strike a winner.

Pete Sampras is who I would consider the greates server of all time, not only because is 1st serve was very powerful, but also because his second serve was the best we've ever seen. Other great servers include tall players, such as John Isner and Ivo Karlovic and players with great serving technique and disguise such as Roger Federer and Novak Djokovic.


Slice and Volley

Slicing and Volleying lost some popularity and effectiveness during the mid-2000s, due to the slowing of the courts and more emphasis on rallying. A slive is, as the name implies, a stroke where the motion is akin to that of striking a sword, slicing the ball. It is also known as a 'chip'.

Volleying is when a player is close to the net, and strikes the ball without it bouncing on the court. It is a very attacking stroke, and the objective should be to finish the point.

Some of the greatest volleyers and slice users include:

  • John Mcenroe
  • Boris Becker
  • Pete Sampras
  • Roger Federer
